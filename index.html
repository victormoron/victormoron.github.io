Why I do this work

My path has been shaped by transformation, impact and the challenge of aligning technology with what matters

I’m driven by a simple idea: technology should deliver real outcomes for people, not just technical milestones. That belief led me from deep scientific research into AI strategy, where innovation, ethics and execution intersect.

I started in research because I loved solving hard problems. But over time, I realized that what mattered more wasn’t the solution itself, it was what the solution changed. That insight pulled me out of academia and into data science, and eventually into leadership, where I could move beyond building models to shaping how AI actually impacts people, products and decisions.

Today, I focus on helping organizations turn AI from potential into performance. That means making sure systems are technically strong, strategically aligned and trusted to deliver in the real world. I help connect data, execution and intent, so AI delivers not just capability, but reliable, meaningful outcomes. Because in the end, what determines success isn’t just what AI can do, but how responsibly and effectively it’s applied.

I see the future of AI leadership as deeply cross-functional, where technical fluency, ethical grounding and strategic clarity must coexist. If you're working on something meaningful with AI, or navigating its complexity, I'd love to connect.
