A small set of representative case studies from the last five years. Employer names are kept private where appropriate; outcomes, methods, and metrics are real.

01 — TEAM BUILDING
A global media data business · 300+ people

Building a global data science team from one to fifteen

Senior Director, Data Science · 2021–2025

The brief

The business was standing up a new data-products line and had one data scientist. To meet demand across EMEA and the US, the function needed to grow — and grow in a way that could ship products end-to-end, not just produce analyses someone else would have to operationalise.

What I did

I hired the team from one person to fifteen across EMEA and the US in six months — but the harder half of the job was deciding what they did. I designed the roles, the ownership models, and the ways of working across data science, engineering, and product, so capabilities could be shipped end-to-end rather than handed over a wall.

Alongside that, I built a culture of open challenge and continuous learning. Disagreement was expected. Mistakes got post-mortemed in the open, not buried. That's the part you can't put on an org chart — but it's the part that made the rest hold.

Outcome

Most of the team were promoted within two years. The function stayed productive through a major restructuring, a leadership transition, and the entire shift to cookieless — and the methodologies it produced powered the synthetic-data, data-fusion, and imputation work this team went on to ship.

02 — SYNTHETIC DATA
A global media data business · 300+ people

A digital twin of 300M+ synthetic people for cookieless geo-targeting

Senior Director, Data Science · 2021–2025

The brief

The deprecation of third-party cookies put the entire planning lifecycle under pressure. The business needed a way to do geo-targeting and audience discovery without relying on signals that were disappearing.

What I did

I extended data-fusion methodologies my team had developed into geospatial and synthetic-data generation — producing a digital-twin dataset describing 300M+ synthetic people. This gave planners a privacy-preserving substrate for cookieless targeting that could be reasoned about, audited, and reused across products.

I designed the automation that let the solution be deployed market-by-market, then drove the adoption work — internal enablement, training, and integration with the planning tools people already used.

Outcome

The dataset became core infrastructure for cookieless planning at the business, used daily by planners across the organisation.

03 — PLATFORM
A global media data business · 300+ people

A data fusion platform that opened £10M+ in new revenue

Senior Director, Data Science · 2021–2025

The brief

Client data and proprietary datasets sat in separate worlds. Bringing them together took bespoke effort each time — slow, expensive, and impossible to scale.

What I did

I built a data fusion platform integrating client data into proprietary datasets — first as a delivery capability, then evolved into a reusable platform with consistent interfaces, governance, and operational tooling.

I worked across data science, engineering, and product to codify the fusion methodology, automate the heavy lifting, and design the commercial model that turned every deal into a building block for the next one.

Outcome

The platform opened entirely new revenue streams while collapsing the cost and time of delivery — a rare combination, and the one that turns a project into a business line.

04 — METHODOLOGY
A global media data business · 300+ people

Automating survey reconstruction across 79+ markets

Senior Director, Data Science · 2021–2025

The brief

A proprietary survey dataset underpinned a substantial part of the business — but it was costly to run and slow to refresh in new markets, with partial responses limiting what could be done with the data.

What I did

I designed an automated multiple-imputation pipeline combining MICE, hot-deck, and statistical synthesis to reconstruct full respondent profiles from partial responses — turning a manual, expert-heavy process into a repeatable system.

Outcome

Better data, dramatically lower costs, and a step-change in how quickly the business could expand into new markets.

05 — PRODUCT
A major UK retail loyalty platform

A data-driven campaign management platform replacing fragmented manual workflows

Lead Data Scientist · 2019–2021

The brief

Offer planning across commercial and marketing teams was fragmented — manual workflows, inconsistent targeting, and no good way to coordinate thousands of customers and multiple simultaneous offer cycles.

What I did

I architected an end-to-end campaign management platform from concept to delivery — centralising and automating offer planning, with recommendation-based targeting at the core. Alongside it, I developed models across NLP, customer behaviour change detection, targeting, and attribution using one of the UK's largest loyalty datasets.

To make any of this shippable, I worked with engineering and DevOps to modernise the ML deployment pipeline — taking model deployment from days to hours.

Outcome

A consolidated platform replacing siloed manual processes, with measurable improvements in targeting and operational throughput.

06 — GOVERNANCE
A global media data business · 300+ people

Defining Responsible AI from scratch — and catching a flawed model before it shipped

VP of Quality & Responsible AI · 2025–2026

The brief

Stand up a Responsible AI practice across a 300-person data product organisation during a period of company restructuring — and make it work end-to-end, from initial model design through to client deployment.

What I did

I designed a gated AI risk framework embedded into the product development lifecycle, with structured documentation ingested into a centralised system. The framework gave governance, data science, and stakeholder teams a single source of truth — auditable, analysable, and aligned to the way the business actually shipped products.

In parallel, I extended earlier synthetic-data research into practical LLM and synthetic-data guidelines for 150+ planners across the wider group, so generative AI could be adopted responsibly inside live client projects.

I also identified an architectural gap between model-level governance and the new generation of multi-model agentic systems, scoped the technical approach for extending the framework to system-level oversight, and secured executive endorsement to prioritise it as the next phase of the roadmap.

Outcome

Within the first months of operation, the framework caught a critical modelling failure: a data enrichment model that was replicating survey patterns rather than capturing real online behavioural signals — preventing a flawed dataset from reaching live client environments.
