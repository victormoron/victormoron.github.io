What I believe

01

No real reason, no real project.

Trends aren't strategy and "Because everyone else is doing it" isn't a reason. Every project should start with a problem worth solving, and a case worth defending.

02

Tech is the medium. People are the point.

Every AI system serves someone: the user, the team operating it, the leadership accountable for it. The technology matters but the people matter more.

03

Simple first.

Real simplicity is hard to build. If it doesn't feel simple, it isn't finished.

From quantum models to real-world impact

I started in computational chemistry because I loved solving hard problems. After years in academia building quantum models on high-performance compute clusters — work that produced 8 peer-reviewed papers and over 15 international conference presentations — I became more interested in how those skills could move the needle outside the lab.

That insight pulled me into industry, where I could build products that made decisions better, fairer, or faster.

Building data & AI organisations

Over the last decade I've built data science and AI capability from scratch — three times. I led data science at a major UK loyalty programme through a period of rapid modernisation. I then hired and grew a global data science team from 1 to 15 across EMEA and the US at a global media data business, designing the methodologies that gave the business the data it needed when that data didn't exist.

Most recently, I defined the Responsible AI function for the same business: a gated risk framework embedded into the product development lifecycle, applied across 4 verticals and 45 data scientists. In its first months it caught a flawed enrichment model before it reached client environments, and produced the practical LLM and synthetic-data guidelines now used by 150+ planners across the group.
